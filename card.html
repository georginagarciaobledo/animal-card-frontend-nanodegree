Tardigrade

Tardigrades can withstand temperatures ranging from -378 to over 300 degrees Fahrenheit.

  • Scientific Name: Tardigrada (Phylum; more than 1000 tardigrade species have been identified)
  • Average Length: 0.5 mm
  • Average Lifespan: A few months when continuously hydrated
  • Habitat: Varied

These tiny invertebrates are perhaps the toughest life forms we know. They don't like living in extreme conditions (they are not extremophiles), but are able to survive in incredibly challenging conditions. They can even survive in outer space! They are able to withstand extreme conditions by drying themselves out when necessary and rehydrating themselves when possible. Tardigrades are also called water bears or moss piglets.
